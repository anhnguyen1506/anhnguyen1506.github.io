Furniture Pieces


____________________________



Pieces from different time periods are combined: Art Deco, French antiques, classic French curved pieces like the classic Bergere chair, the cabriole sofa or settee, an armoire, a Louis XV chair, antique trunks, as well as ultra-contemporary pieces.

Materials


____________________________



A variety of high quality wood is used in this style. Regarding fabric, velvet and leather are common. For a more casual vibe, linen and cotton are also used.

Lighting


____________________________



French empire crystal chandeliers are great for this style. Traditional floor lamps, sconces and table lamps also fit in very well.

Rugs


____________________________



Common types of rugs used are silk or wool rugs, natural fiber rugs, such as jute or sisal and zebra rug.


Window Treatments


____________________________



Common choices will be floor to ceiling silk, linen, or high quality cotton drapes. Flat roman shades can also be used for a simple, natural look.

Common Elements


____________________________



The most distinctive trait of Modern French Style is the dramatic element - in each room there should be something remarable to attract attention and keep things interesting.

Floorings


____________________________



Wood, parquet and elaborate tile floors are the most common choices. Flooring in the entrance of the home is usually more elaborate, with marble, mosaic or stone.


Paintings


____________________________



Paintings used in Modern French home are usually classic works of art in gold frames, or large abstract paintings. Children’s’ illustrations or pop art may also fit in well.

Accessories


____________________________



Common accessories for this style include: gilded gold mirrors, French bar cart, elaborate crown molding or coffered ceilings. These will add a classical touch to this modern, chic-looking style.
