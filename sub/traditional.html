Furniture Pieces


____________________________



In Traditional Interior Design, common furniture include curved pieces, wingback chairs, chesterfield sofa, claw-footed tables, Queen Anne, Chippendale or Sheraton pieces.

Materials


____________________________



A variety of dark wood is used in this style, including mahogany, walnut, maple and cherry woods. Regarding fabric, silk, cashmere, velvet, and damask are common. For a more casual vibe, linen and cotton are also used.

Rugs


____________________________



Oriental, Persian, or Kilim rugs - those with a vintage, almost antique look - fit in perfectly.

Lighting


____________________________



Crystal chandeliers are great for this style. Furniture or fixtures with warm tones finishes such as bronze, worn brass or copper with white, ivory, or black lamp shades also fit in very well.


Window Treatments


____________________________



Common choices include drapery panels that are either straight or pleated with embellishments like tassels, fringe, tiebacks and valances. Roman shades or simple plantation shutters can also be used.

Floorings


____________________________



Wood, laminate, tile or carpeting are the most common choices.

Common Elements


____________________________



The most distinctive trait of Traditional Interior Design is the extensive use of Formal symmetry, arches, columns, crown molding, wall molding, and wainscoting,


Paintings


____________________________



Paintings used in Traditional home are usually reproductions of classic pieces from artists such as Rembrandt, Van Gogh, Renoir, Da Vinci and Monet. Norman Rockwell's works are also pretty common.

Accessories


____________________________



Collections of objects can really make a Traditional home unique and sophisticated. Anything from vintage records, teapots, books or miniatures will do the job.
